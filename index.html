Biography

I am a Research Fellow at the European University Institute, Florence within the framework of the SOLID project led by Prof. Hanspeter Kriesi. My current work within the SOLID project hosted at the EUI focuses on collective mobilization, public opinion, and party competition dynamics for the study of the multiple crises that have hit the EU since 2008. My broader research interests include political behavior, political participation and mobilization, political representation, and public opinion formation. Methodologically, I focus on text as data solutions for semi-automated protest event analysis, surveys and survey experiments, and QCA.

I have completed my PhD in Comparative Politics at Central European University in Budapest on policy responsiveness to collective mobilization. I am main developer of the R package SetMethods (~30,000 CRAN downloads) and have extensive experience in teaching research design, R, and QCA at various international methods schools and universities (ECPR Methods Schools, Lund University, University of Helsinki, EUI, etc.). My book, co-authored with Carsten Q. Schneider and Eva Thomann, ‘Qualitative Comparative Analysis (QCA) using R: A Gentle Introduction’ has just been published with Cambridge University Press.
